If you want to appear in Google AI Overviews, ChatGPT, Perplexity, or Gemini today, classic SEO alone isn't enough. You need to think like both a traditional search engine and a system that answers the user's question directly. The goal is no longer just to rank — it's to be the source they cite.

## How AI Engines Read Your Site

AI systems look for three main things: a clear answer to a clear question, clean structure, and credibility that signals expertise. AI doesn't want long text — it wants a precise answer inside long text.

## What to Do to Appear in AI Answers

To build organic presence in AI engines, there are five main steps.

**First** — Build answer pages. For each core topic in your business, create one page that covers it in depth: a clear headline as a question or definition, an opening paragraph of 80–120 words that sums up the answer directly, 5–10 subheadings each with a specific question and focused answer, and an FAQ section at the end. The goal is for each part to stand alone as a citable answer.

**Second** — Write in Q&A structure. Every H2 should be a real question someone would ask in chat, with a full but focused answer of 100–300 words below it. Don't write long intros before answering.

**Third** — Build content clusters. Choose 3–5 areas you want to be known for, and for each build a central pillar page with 4–8 supporting articles and clear internal links. This signals to AI that you're not a single article but a consistent knowledge source.

**Fourth** — Strengthen credibility. Show real author name, role, and relevant experience, plus a clear "About" page. Include real cases and data — AI tends to cite content with depth, not just generic wording.

**Fifth** — Keep technical foundation clean: proper heading structure, good load speed, HTTPS, Sitemap, and Schema where needed. If the site is slow or messy, it's harder to crawl and understand.

## What Not to Do

<Callout>
  AI spots shallow content quickly. Generic content without focus on a specific question, generic text without editing and real examples, mixing unrelated topics on the same page — all hurt your chance of being cited.
</Callout>
